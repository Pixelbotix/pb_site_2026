Modes of Collaboration

Structured engagement models aligned to execution depth, duration, and institutional or industry objectives.

Short-term Programs

High-impact, short-duration engagements focused on rapid knowledge transfer, exposure, and immediate skill enhancement.

  • Technical workshops
  • Guest lectures & expert talks
  • Bootcamps & demo sessions

Medium-term Engagements

Structured programs spanning weeks or months, enabling deeper capability building, mentorship, and hands-on execution.

  • Faculty Development Programs (FDPs)
  • Student project mentorship
  • Internship programs
  • Lab enablement & curriculum support

Long-term Partnerships

Strategic, multi-year collaborations focused on sustained impact, joint ownership, and ecosystem-level outcomes.

  • Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs)
  • Joint academic or industry programs
  • Recurring training & upskilling initiatives
  • Collaborative applied R&D

Partner Expectations

Clear engagement principles to ensure alignment, execution quality, and meaningful long-term collaboration.

Commitment to Execution

Engagements must be outcome-driven, with clarity on objectives, timelines, ownership, and decision-making authority from the partner side.

Openness to Applied Learning

Partners should actively support hands-on execution, practical exposure, and learning-by-doing rather than symbolic participation.

Structured Collaboration

Defined coordination, institutional backing, and continuity across departments are mandatory for success.

Ethical & Academic Integrity

All collaborations must align with academic integrity, ethical conduct, and long-term learner or industry value.
